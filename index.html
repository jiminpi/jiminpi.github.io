About Me

I'm a senior PhD student at Hong Kong University of Science and Technology (HKUST), Hong Kong, supervised by Prof. Bertram Shi . Before that, I received my Bachelor degree from the Huazhong University of Science and Technology (HUST).

My research focus on intelligent computer/robot system interaction. Specifically, I am actively developing intelligent gaze-based interaction for the disabled by referring to context. I think this is an exciting field because it has immediate applications. For instance, in one of my recent research projects, I had the opportunity to collaborate with public hospitals in Hong Kong to test our recently proposed eye typing technique, and we found that the users had an increase in typing speed of about 50% compared to previous techniques. Projects

Gaze-based Text Input Communication System for the Disabled

  • From Mar 2016 to present.
  • Implementation of a text input interface using gaze.
  • Proposed a probabilistic generative model for gaze.
  • Used Bayesian to integrate gaze cue and context knowledge.
  • Achieved 50% typing speed increase compared to previous techniques.
  • Tested with (Spinal cord injuries) patients in Hong Kong hospitals.

    3D Gaze Estimation Using Eye Glasses

  • From Jul 2017 to Apr 2018.
  • Estimating human pose through SLAM algorithm.
  • Localizing 3D gaze based on the 3D environment context.
  • Achieved accuracy of 3 degrees in a region of over 2m*2m.

    Online Calibration for Remote Eye Trackers

  • From Aug 2018 to Mar 2019.
  • Investigated eye tracking degradation through empirical study.
  • Proposed a calibration mechanism based on the natural human-computer interaction history.
  • Solved the calibration using an efficient convex optimization scheme.
  • Reduced errors by up to 43% as the head moves over a 20cm range
